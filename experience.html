Android App Development Intern
Summer 2020

 Audition Technology is a start up focused on hearing health devices and applications. I worked as an Android App Development Intern for their app focused on keeping users informed about how much sound they are exposed to daily, so they can be aware of their potential risks for hearing loss.
  I implemented the audio streaming exposure feature, where the user can get an estimated volume of the media they are currently streaming if they are using earbuds, headphones, etc. Also, I worked to improve an already established ambient exposure feature, where the app uses the phone microphone to measure the volume of the user's environment. Specifically I added the ability for the user to take a 5 second measurement of their ambient noise, then use a slider to input how many hours they will be in the current location, so the app can have estimates of their daily exposure without them having to keep the app running 24/7 and draining their battery. Along with the manual measurment, I added the automatic measurement feature, where the user can have the app run indefinately in the background and it will automatically take a 5 second sample every hour, so the user does not have to remember to manually tell the app to take an ambient exposure sample. Lastly, the app also has an exposure graph displaying the user's daily cumulative exposure in a line graph. I worked on adding a gradient fill to the exposure graph with the lower parts starting at a green color, then depening on the how close the user is close to hitting their daily recommended sound exposure threshold, the graph will turn yellow, and eventually red.

Daedalus's Creation (Class Project)
December 2018

  Daedalus's Creation is a 3D first person pov maze game. I made this game for the 15-112 Fundamentals of Programming class, using Panda3D - an outdated 3D rendering platform - choosing 3D Panda since the requirement was that the project has to be in Python, the language we learned in class. I wanted to try out a platform I had never used before, and decided to make a game based off of the greek myth of the Minotaur. The player is put into a randomly generated maze, and is trying to save people inside maze from the Minotaur. Once the player finds and kills the Minotaur a golden rope appears, leading them out of the maze. The largest struggle I had while creating this game was the fact that bug fixing was much harder than usual, since no one uses Panda3D there were very few answers to be found in online forums and documentation. I also had to integrate textures with different rendering platforms which I had no experience with.

Manus Defense (Hackathon Project)
December 2018

 Manus Defense was a project my friends and I made for our 15-112 class hackathon. We had 24 hours and decided that we wanted to somehow incorporate a piece of hardware into our project, so we picked up a hand and wrist position sensor, Leap Motion. The game is simple, protect the star in the middle of the screen from red asteroids trying to hit it by rotating the semi circle shields around the star with your hand. Our team of four brainstormed together then each worked on our own parts of the game, making sure our individual classes could be put together soundly via differing attributes. I worked on the star and shield classes, while my teammates developed the asteroid class, integrated data from the Leap Motion device, and added music and sound effects. We constantly discussed our progress and design along the way to make sure everything would work as one unit.

Argus (Hackathon Project)
September 2018

 Argus was the first hackathon project I ever did, at the very beginning of freshman year. Admittedly I did not know anything about machine learning at the time, but two of my teammates wanted to use TensorFlow to train a model and we wanted our project to fall on the side of social good. So we settled on Argus, an ios app that helps the vision impaired safely cross walkways by telling them outloud whether it is safe to cross. We trained a model that detects whether the crosswalk sign is 'stop' with a red hand, or 'go' with a white walking person. As seen in the demo, it ended up being fairly accurate. Though I did not personally contribute a lot of code to this project, I did learn a lot of things from my teammates on an area I had no knowledge about. The process of seeing the model being trained and how it behaved based on our test data gave me first hand experience I can draw upon and connect to when I run into topics in the realm of machine learning.
